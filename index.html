    A Little History

    The Pomodoro Technique was created by Francesco Cirillo, an Italian developer and entrepreneur, in the late 1980s while he was a university student struggling to stay focused on his studies. He named it after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer ("pomodoro" means tomato in Italian) he used to break his work into short, focused intervals, and the method has since grown into one of the most popular tools for fighting procrastination and managing time.

    How to Use This App

    1. Add a task and set how many pomodoros you think it will take.
    2. Tap Select on that task so the timer can track its progress.
    3. Pick a mode: Focus, Short Break, or Long Break.
    4. Press Start and work (or rest) until the timer hits zero.
    5. Take the break, then start your next focus session — every 4th one gives a longer break.
    6. Check Your Progress for today's pomodoro count and your streak.
